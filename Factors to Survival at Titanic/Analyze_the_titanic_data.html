Analyze Titanic Data¶

What factors are coorelate to people's survival? Class, Gender or others?¶

Project Summary¶

This is a dataset on the passengers's information in Tatinic Accident. I am intersted in understanding a simple question. What factors are related to people's survival? Were women more likely to survive? Were the wealthy more likely to survive? Did they give the youth more chance to get on the rescue boats? Below are my assumption and analysis.

Dataset description¶

The dataset contains seven variables for 891 observations. The 12 variables are:

    • Survived: Survival (0=No,1=Yes)
    • passengerID
    • Pclass Passenger class (Survived 1 = 1st; 2 = 2nd; 3 = 3rd)
    • Name
    • Sex
    • Age
    • sibsp: Number of Siblings/Spouses Aboard
    • parch: Number of Parents/Children Aboard
    • ticket: Ticket Number
    • fare: Passenger Fare
    • cabin: Cabin
    • embarked: Port of Embarkation(C = Cherbourg; Q = Queenstown; S = Southampton)

    I found the dataset very clean when I did descrptive analysis. To explore the gender factor more quickly, I decided to add an indicator "Sexindex", where it equals to 1 for female and equals to 0 for male passenger. I plot some figures to present the major variables in the dataset as follows.

Assumption and Research Questions¶

We assume gender, age and class might be related to the chance of survival. Number of Siblings/Spourses Aboard and Number of Parents/Children Aboard might be corelated to the survival rate too, as younger people and female passengers tend to have families onboard.

  • What's the demographics of survivals different from those who died in this tragety in terms of sex, age and class? (Who survived and who died?)
  • What are the top 3 factors that are related to the chance of survival? Are these factors correlated?

    Methodology:¶

    1. We first identify the demographics differences between the survived and deceased group
    2. To find the answer to the second question, we test the survival rate difference by groups due to different factors.
    3. I conducted t and f test to determine whether these factors are statitstically significant
    4. Using covariance analysis, top 3 factors are found.

    • Step 1. Identify the demographics difference between the survived and deceased groups¶

    We examined the dataset and decided to focus on the five factors: Sex, Age, Pclass, Parch and SibSp. We did not dive into the other factors either because they are strongly correlated to existing factor (such as fare and Pclass) or the data of that variable is incomplete (e.g. Carbin) or unrelated (e.g. Embarked)

    Comparing the survived and deceased group among the five factors, we found the following

    1) The proportion of female in the deceased group is much lower; 2) more people in more people survived in the first passenger class than other classes, though the total number of passenger in that class is fewest. 3) In terms of age, the average age of survived group is smaller than deceased group. Age might also be a determinstics. 4) The survived have more parents/children traveling with them on average 5) The average of SibSp is not much different between the survived and deceased groups

    Step 3. Testing the key factors - are they statistically significant?¶

    I use independent t test to test whether the difference between survival rate between groups by the key factors "Age_group"and "Sex" are significant. We 'Pclass'.

    Sex - significant¶

    We set confidence level as 95%. Since we don't know the population mean, and the sample is normally distributed. We will use independ t test to test the following hypothesis.

    The survival rate is higher if female.

    M_f is the mean survival rate of people who are no younger than 16. M_m is the mean survival rate of people who are younger than 16. Null hypothese is M_f=M_m; Alternative hypothese is M_f>M_m; The t critical value at confidence level of 95% at df(889) is -1.645. Since t-value is -18.67 << 1.645, we reject the null hypothesis. It is statistically significant that the survial rate of female is higher.

    In [325]:
    def calculate_t(variable):
    count=data.groupby(variable)['Survived'].count()
    mean=data.groupby(variable)['Survived'].mean()
    std=data.groupby(variable)['Survived'].std()
    t=(mean[0]-mean[1])/sqrt((std[0]/sqrt(count[0]))**2+(std[1]/sqrt(count[1]))**2)
    return t

    Pclass - significant¶

    Since we only have one factor Pclass on the survial rate of three group, we use one variable F-test to test the following hypothesis: The survival rate of three groups are differnet The null hypothesis is "the survaival rate among the three group is the same". The alternative hypothesis is "the survaival rate among the three group is different".

    The F value at df1=1 and df2=891-2 at 95% confidence level (alpha=0.05) is 2.9957. Our F_value is 57.75>>2.9957, so we reject the null hypothesis. The survival rate is significantly different by Pclass.

    Conclusion¶

    Through the analysis on Titanic dataset, we identify three key factors to the survial: gender, class and age(whether under 16). We are lucky enough today to attain this historic data and conduct our data exploration. However, the analysis has limitation due to data availability. For example, some observations have missing values in certain fields such as age, we omited the missing value and it might cause inaccurancy. On the other hand, there might be other factor that could be important to the survival but not included in this dataset such as distance from the cabin to the exit and the floor of their rooms. It would be interesting to look at how the location affect the survival rate.
